# Getting started

timedart is a local-first app for tracking billable time and turning it into
invoices. Your data lives on your device — no account required.

> **Note:** A new install starts with a small **example** — one client, two
> projects, and some tasks and time entries — so you can see how tracking looks
> before adding your own. It's ordinary data: explore it freely, then when
> you're ready for the real thing, **delete the "Example Client"** (timedart
> will offer to remove it and everything under it in one step) or **archive** it
> to tuck it away while keeping it around. See the **Your data** page for the
> difference between archiving and deleting.

## How your work is organised

timedart uses a simple hierarchy:

- **Client:** a person or organisation you bill. Owns projects and a default hourly rate.
- **Project:** a body of work for one client, with an optional rate override and a reference code.
- **Task:** a named unit of work inside a project.
- **Time entry:** a single tracked interval against a task — a start, a duration, and an optional note.

A rate set lower down wins: a task uses its own rate if it has one, otherwise its
project's, otherwise the client's default.

## Track your first entry

1. Add a **client** and give it a default rate.
2. Add a **project** under that client.
3. Add a **task** to the project.
4. Select the task and press **Start**. The timer runs — you can pause and resume it.
5. Press **Finish** to save the interval as a **time entry**.

> **Note:** A running timer survives quitting and reopening the app — it resumes
> where you left off, so you never lose in-progress time to a restart.

## Turn time into an invoice

Once a project has time entries, open its **Invoice** view to preview a branded
invoice and export it as a PDF. What appears on the invoice — your business
details, logo, payment fields, and styling — comes from your **profile** and its
**template**, which you set up once in Settings.
